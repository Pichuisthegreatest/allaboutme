Derivatives

Calculus - Not by Pichu E [Note: Pichu E is so smart]

Derivative = dy/dx (if you see that, derive yourself)

Shows the gradient of a curve

Example a)
y = x² + 4x

  • Rewrite as x² + 4x¹
  • Multiply x by powers: 2x² + 4x¹
  • Take 1 from powers: 2x¹ + 4x⁰ = 2x + 4
  • Hence, gradient at y = x² + 4x at point (3,10):
    2x + 4 = 2(3) + 4 = 10

Example b)
x5/6

  • Multiply x by powers: (5/6)x5/6
  • Subtract 1 from powers: (5/6)x-1/6

Example c)
1/3x⁵

  • Rewrite as x-5/3
  • Power rule (subtract 1): -5x-6/3 or -5/3x-6
  • Power rule always subtracts 1, it will go further negative.

Integrals

Integral = ∫

  • If an integral has numbers (eg: ∫34) it has limits.
  • If you integrate without limits, add '+C' or lose a mark.
  • Integration is the opposite of derivation: add one to the power, then divide by the new power.

Example a)
∫2x + 4

  • Rewrite in x terms: ∫2x¹ + 4x⁰
  • 2x divided by power + 1: 2x/2 = x
  • Add 1 to power: x²
  • Repeat with 4x⁰: 4x/1 = 4x
  • Final answer: x² + 4x

Example b) With limits
∫12 2x + 4

  • Integrate to get x² + 4x
  • Subtract equation with x=top from x=bottom:
    ((2)² + 4(2)) - ((1)² + 4(1)) = 12 - 5 = 7
